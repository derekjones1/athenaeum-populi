## Recognize the identity properties of addition and multiplication

What happens when we add zero to any number? Adding zero doesn't change the
value. For this reason, we call $0$ the **additive identity**. For example,

$$13 + 0 = 13 \qquad -14 + 0 = -14 \qquad 0 + (-3x) = -3x$$

What happens when you multiply any number by one? Multiplying by one
doesn't change the value. So we call $1$ the **multiplicative identity**.
For example,

$$
43 \cdot 1 = 43 \qquad -27 \cdot 1 = -27 \qquad 1 \cdot \tfrac{6y}{5} =
\tfrac{6y}{5}
$$

{{< callout type="info" >}}
  **Identity properties.**

  The identity property of addition: for any real number $a$,

  $$a + 0 = a \qquad\qquad 0 + a = a$$

  $0$ is called the **additive identity**.

  The identity property of multiplication: for any real number $a$,

  $$a \cdot 1 = a \qquad\qquad 1 \cdot a = a$$

  $1$ is called the **multiplicative identity**.
{{< /callout >}}

**Example.** Identify whether each equation demonstrates the identity
property of addition or multiplication: (a) $7 + 0 = 7$ (b) $-16(1) =
-16$.

(a) We are adding $0$, so this uses the identity property of addition.

(b) We are multiplying by $1$, so this uses the identity property of
multiplication.

## Use the inverse properties of addition and multiplication

What number added to $5$ gives the additive identity, $0$? We know $5 +
(-5) = 0$. What number added to $-6$ gives $0$? We know $-6 + 6 = 0$.
Notice that in each case, the missing number was the *opposite* of the
number.

We call $-a$ the **additive inverse** of $a$. The opposite of a number is
its additive inverse. A number and its opposite add to $0$, which is the
additive identity.

What number multiplied by $\tfrac{2}{3}$ gives the multiplicative
identity, $1$? We know $\tfrac{2}{3} \cdot \tfrac{3}{2} = 1$. What number
multiplied by $2$ gives $1$? We know $2 \cdot \tfrac{1}{2} = 1$. Notice
that in each case, the missing number was the *reciprocal* of the number.

We call $\tfrac{1}{a}$ the **multiplicative inverse** of $a$ (where $a \neq
0$). The reciprocal of a number is its multiplicative inverse. A number
and its reciprocal multiply to $1$, which is the multiplicative identity.

{{< callout type="info" >}}
  **Inverse properties.**

  Inverse Property of Addition: for any real number $a$,

  $$a + (-a) = 0$$

  $-a$ is the **additive inverse** of $a$.

  Inverse Property of Multiplication: for any real number $a \neq 0$,

  $$a \cdot \tfrac{1}{a} = 1$$

  $\tfrac{1}{a}$ is the **multiplicative inverse** of $a$.
{{< /callout >}}

**Example.** Find the additive inverse of each expression: (a) $13$ (b)
$-\tfrac{5}{8}$ (c) $0.6$.

To find the additive inverse, we find the opposite: (a) the additive
inverse of $13$ is $-13$ (b) the additive inverse of $-\tfrac{5}{8}$ is
$\tfrac{5}{8}$ (c) the additive inverse of $0.6$ is $-0.6$.

  answer="-18"
  hint="The additive inverse is the opposite of the number."
>}}

  answer="-1.2"
  hint="The additive inverse is the opposite of the number."

**Example.** Find the multiplicative inverse: (a) $9$ (b) $-\tfrac{1}{9}$
(c) $0.9$.

To find the multiplicative inverse, we find the reciprocal: (a) the

inverse of $-\tfrac{1}{9}$ is $-9$ (c) to find the multiplicative inverse
of $0.9$, we first convert it to a fraction, $\tfrac{9}{10}$, then find the

## Use the properties of zero

We have already learned that zero is the additive identity, since it can
be added to any number without changing the number's identity. But zero
also has some special properties when it comes to multiplication and
division.

### Multiplication by zero

What happens when you multiply a number by $0$? Multiplying by $0$ makes
the product equal zero. The product of any real number and $0$ is $0$.

{{< callout type="info" >}}
  **Multiplication by zero.** For any real number $a$,

  $$a \cdot 0 = 0 \qquad\qquad 0 \cdot a = 0$$
{{< /callout >}}

**Example.** Simplify: (a) $-8 \cdot 0$ (b) $\tfrac{5}{12} \cdot 0$ (c)
$0(2.94)$.

The product of any real number and $0$ is $0$, so all three expressions
simplify to $0$.

{{< fillin
  question="Simplify: $-14 \cdot 0$."
  answer="0"
  hint="The product of any real number and $0$ is $0$."
>}}

### Dividing with zero

What about dividing with $0$? Think about a real example: if there are no
cookies in the cookie jar and three people want to share them, how many
cookies would each person get? There are $0$ cookies to share, so each
person gets $0$ cookies:

$$0 \div 3 = 0 \text{ because } 0 \cdot 3 = 0$$

{{< callout type="info" >}}
  **Division of zero.** For any real number $a$, except $0$: $\tfrac{0}{a}
  = 0$ and $0 \div a = 0$.
{{< /callout >}}

Zero divided by any real number except zero is zero.

**Example.** Simplify: (a) $0 \div 5$ (b) $\tfrac{0}{-2}$ (c) $0 \div
\tfrac{7}{8}$.

Zero divided by any real number, except $0$, is zero — so all three
expressions simplify to $0$.

{{< fillin
  question="Simplify: $0 \div 11$."
  answer="0"
  hint="Zero divided by any nonzero number is zero."
>}}

Now let's think about dividing a number *by* zero. What is the result of
dividing $4$ by $0$? Think about the related multiplication fact — is
there a number that multiplied by $0$ gives $4$? Since any real number
multiplied by $0$ equals $0$, there is no real number that can be
multiplied by $0$ to obtain $4$. We can conclude that there is no answer
to $4 \div 0$, and so we say that division by zero is **undefined**.

{{< callout type="info" >}}
  **Division by zero.** For any real number $a$: $\tfrac{a}{0}$ and $a \div
  0$ are undefined.
{{< /callout >}}

Division *by* zero is undefined.

**Example.** Simplify: (a) $7.5 \div 0$ (b) $\tfrac{-32}{0}$ (c)
$\tfrac{4}{9} \div 0$.

Division by zero is undefined, so all three expressions are undefined.

{{< fillin
  question="How many of these four expressions are undefined: $0 \div 11$, $16.4 \div 0$, $\tfrac{-2}{0}$, and $\tfrac{0}{-6}$?"
  answer="2"
  hint="Zero divided by a nonzero number equals $0$ (defined). Any nonzero number divided by zero is undefined."
>}}

We summarize the properties of zero:

{{< callout type="info" >}}
  **Properties of zero.**

  Multiplication by Zero: for any real number $a$, $a \cdot 0 = 0$ and $0
  \cdot a = 0$ — the product of any number and $0$ is $0$.

  Division by Zero: for any real number $a \neq 0$, $\tfrac{0}{a} = 0$
  (zero divided by any real number, except itself, is zero), but
  $\tfrac{a}{0}$ is undefined (division by zero is undefined).
{{< /callout >}}

## Simplify expressions using the properties of identities, inverses, and zero

We will now practice using the properties of identities, inverses, and
zero to simplify expressions.

**Example.** Simplify: $3x + 15 - 3x$.

Notice the additive inverses, $3x$ and $-3x$; they combine to $0$, leaving
the identity:

$$3x + 15 - 3x = (3x - 3x) + 15 = 0 + 15 = 15$$

{{< fillin
  question="Simplify: $-12z + 9 + 12z$."
  answer="9"
  hint="$-12z$ and $12z$ are additive inverses — they combine to $0$, leaving the identity property."
>}}

**Example.** Simplify: $4(0.25q)$.

Regroup using the associative property, then multiply — the two factors
are reciprocals, so the coefficient becomes the multiplicative identity:

$$4(0.25q) = [4(0.25)]q = 1.00q = q$$

{{< fillin
  question="Simplify: $2(0.5p)$."
  answer="p"
  hint="$2$ and $0.5$ are reciprocals — regroup and multiply them first; the result is the multiplicative identity."
>}}

**Example.** Simplify: $\tfrac{0}{n+5}$, where $n \neq -5$.

Zero divided by any real number except itself is zero:

$$\tfrac{0}{n+5} = 0$$

{{< fillin
  question="Simplify: $\tfrac{0}{m + 7}$, where $m \ne -7$."
  answer="0"
  hint="Zero divided by any nonzero expression is zero."
>}}

**Example.** Simplify: $\tfrac{10 - 3p}{0}$.

Division by zero is undefined, so this expression is undefined — no matter
what $p$ is, the denominator is always $0$.

**Example.** Simplify: $\tfrac{3}{4} \cdot \tfrac{4}{3}(6x + 12)$.

We cannot combine the terms in parentheses, so we multiply the two
fractions first — they are reciprocals, so their product is the
multiplicative identity:

$$\tfrac{3}{4} \cdot \tfrac{4}{3}(6x + 12) = 1(6x + 12) = 6x + 12$$

{{< fillin
  question="Simplify: $\tfrac{2}{5} \cdot \tfrac{5}{2}(20y + 50)$."
  answer="20y + 50"
  hint="$\tfrac{2}{5}$ and $\tfrac{5}{2}$ are reciprocals — their product is $1$, the multiplicative identity."
>}}

All the properties of real numbers used in this chapter are summarized
below:

| Property | Of Addition | Of Multiplication |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Commutative Property** — if $a$ and $b$ are real numbers, then... | $a + b = b + a$ | $a \cdot b = b \cdot a$ |
| **Associative Property** — if $a$, $b$, $c$ are real numbers, then... | $(a + b) + c = a + (b + c)$ | $(a \cdot b) \cdot c = a \cdot (b \cdot c)$ |
| **Identity Property** | $0$ is the additive identity: $a + 0 = a$, $0 + a = a$ | $1$ is the multiplicative identity: $a \cdot 1 = a$, $1 \cdot a = a$ |
| **Inverse Property** | $-a$ is the additive inverse of $a$: $a + (-a) = 0$ | for $a \neq 0$, $\tfrac{1}{a}$ is the multiplicative inverse of $a$: $a \cdot \tfrac{1}{a} = 1$ |
| **Properties of Zero** | $a \cdot 0 = 0$, $0 \cdot a = 0$ | for $a \neq 0$: $\tfrac{0}{a} = 0$; $\tfrac{a}{0}$ is undefined |

**Distributive Property:** if $a$, $b$, $c$ are real numbers, then $a(b +
c) = ab + ac$.

## Key terms

**additive identity** — $0$; adding it to any number leaves the number
unchanged. **multiplicative identity** — $1$; multiplying by it leaves any
number unchanged. **additive inverse** — the opposite of a number; a
number and its additive inverse sum to $0$. **multiplicative inverse** —
the reciprocal of a number; a nonzero number and its multiplicative
inverse multiply to $1$. Zero times any number is $0$; zero divided by any
nonzero number is $0$; but division *by* zero is undefined.
