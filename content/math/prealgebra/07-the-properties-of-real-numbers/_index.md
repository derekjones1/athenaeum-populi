You have already worked with counting numbers, whole numbers, integers,
fractions, and decimals. In this chapter, we will fit these together into
the set of real numbers, and learn about the properties that make arithmetic
and algebra work the way they do — properties you will lean on constantly
once you start solving equations.

## Sections

- **Rational and Irrational Numbers** — identifying rational and irrational
  numbers, and classifying numbers as whole, integer, rational, irrational,
  and real.
- **Commutative and Associative Properties** — using the commutative and
  associative properties to simplify expressions, and recognizing when they
  do not apply.
- **Distributive Property** — using the distributive property to simplify
  expressions and to combine like terms.
- **Properties of Identity, Inverses, and Zero** — using the identity and
  inverse properties of addition and multiplication, and the properties of
  zero, to simplify expressions.
- **Systems of Measurement** — converting between units within the U.S.
  system, within the metric system, between the two systems, and converting
  between Fahrenheit and Celsius temperatures.
