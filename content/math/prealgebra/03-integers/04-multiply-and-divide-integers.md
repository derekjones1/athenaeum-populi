## Multiply integers

Since multiplication is mathematical shorthand for repeated addition, the
counter model that worked for addition and subtraction can show
multiplication too. Remember that $a \cdot b$ means add $a$, $b$ times.

$5 \cdot 3$ means add $5$ three times: $5+5+5 = 15$ positives, so
$5 \cdot 3 = 15$. And $-5(3)$ means add $-5$ three times:
$-15$, so $-5(3) = -15$.

Now consider $5(-3)$. This means *subtract* $5$, three times. Thinking of
subtraction as "taking away," this means take away $5$ three times — but
there's nothing to take away, so we start by adding neutral pairs. Taking
away $5$ positives three times (after adding enough neutral pairs) leaves
$15$ negatives: $5(-3) = -15$. Likewise, $(-5)(-3)$ means take away $-5$
three times; after adding neutral pairs and taking away $-5$ three times,
$15$ positives are left: $(-5)(-3) = 15$.

In both cases we started with $15$ neutral pairs; taking away $5$
positives three times left $-15$, while taking away $-5$ three times left
$15$. So:

$$
\begin{array}{l}
5 \cdot 3 = 15 \\
-5(3) = -15 \\
5(-3) = -15 \\
(-5)(-3) = 15
\end{array}
$$

Notice that for multiplication of two signed numbers, when the signs are
the same the product is positive, and when the signs are different the
product is negative.

{{< callout type="info" >}}
  **Multiplication of signed numbers.** Same signs (two positives, or two
  negatives) → positive product. Different signs (positive times
  negative, or negative times positive) → negative product.
{{< /callout >}}

**Example.** Multiply: (a) $-9 \cdot 3$, (b) $-2(-5)$, (c) $4(-8)$,
(d) $7 \cdot 6$.

(a) Different signs, so the product is negative: $-27$.
(b) Same signs, so the product is positive: $10$.
(c) Different signs, so the product is negative: $-32$.
(d) Same signs, so the product is positive: $42$.

{{< fillin

  answer="28"

When we multiply a number by $1$, the result is the same number. What
happens when we multiply by $-1$? Try $-1 \cdot 4 = -4$ (the opposite of
$4$) and $-1(-3) = 3$ (the opposite of $-3$). Each time we multiply a
number by $-1$, we get its opposite.

{{< callout type="info" >}}
  **Multiplication by $-1$.** Multiplying a number by $-1$ gives its
  opposite: $-1a = -a$.
{{< /callout >}}

**Example.** Multiply: (a) $-1 \cdot 7$, (b) $-1(-11)$.

(a) Different signs, so the product is negative — and indeed $-7$ is the
opposite of $7$.
(b) Same signs, so the product is positive — and $11$ is the opposite of
$-11$.

  question="Multiply: $-1 \cdot 9$"
  answer="-9"
  hint="Multiplying by $-1$ gives the opposite of the number."
>}}

{{< fillin
  question="Multiply: $-1 \cdot (-17)$"
  answer="17"
  hint="Multiplying by $-1$ gives the opposite of the number."
>}}

## Divide integers

Division is the inverse operation of multiplication: $15 \div 3 = 5$
because $5 \cdot 3 = 15$. If we look at the multiplication facts above,
we can figure out the rules for dividing integers:

$$
\begin{array}{l}
5 \cdot 3 = 15 \text{ so } 15 \div 3 = 5 \\
(-5)(-3) = 15 \text{ so } 15 \div (-3) = -5 \\
-5(3) = -15 \text{ so } -15 \div 3 = -5 \\
5(-3) = -15 \text{ so } -15 \div (-3) = 5
\end{array}
$$

Division of signed numbers follows the same rules as multiplication: when
the signs are the same the quotient is positive, and when the signs are
different the quotient is negative.

{{< callout type="info" >}}
  **Division of signed numbers.** Same signs (two positives, or two
  negatives) → positive quotient. Different signs → negative quotient.
{{< /callout >}}

Remember that you can always check a division answer by multiplying.

**Example.** Divide: (a) $-27 \div 3$, (b) $-100 \div (-4)$.

(a) Different signs, so the quotient is negative: $-9$.
(b) Same signs, so the quotient is positive: $25$.

{{< fillin
  question="Divide: $-42 \div 6$"
  answer="-7"
  hint="Different signs, so the quotient is negative."
>}}

{{< fillin
  question="Divide: $-117 \div (-3)$"
  answer="39"
  hint="Same signs, so the quotient is positive."
>}}

Just as with multiplication, dividing a number by $1$ leaves it
unchanged. What about dividing by $-1$? Try $8 \div (-1) = -8$ (the
opposite of $8$) and $-9 \div (-1) = 9$ (the opposite of $-9$). Dividing
a number by $-1$ gives its opposite.

{{< callout type="info" >}}
  **Division by $-1$.** Dividing a number by $-1$ gives its opposite:
  $a \div (-1) = -a$.
{{< /callout >}}

**Example.** Divide: (a) $16 \div (-1)$, (b) $-20 \div (-1)$.

(a) The dividend $16$ is divided by $-1$: the signs are different, so
the result is negative, $-16$.
(b) The dividend $-20$ is divided by $-1$: the signs are the same, so
the result is positive, $20$.

  question="Divide: $6 \div (-1)$"
  answer="-6"
  hint="Dividing by $-1$ gives the opposite of the number."
>}}

{{< fillin

  answer="36"
  hint="Dividing by $-1$ gives the opposite of the number."
>}}

## Simplify expressions with integers

Now let's simplify expressions that use all four operations — addition,
subtraction, multiplication, and division — with integers. Remember to
follow the order of operations.

**Example.** Simplify $7(-2) + 4(-7) - 6$. Multiply first:
$-14 + (-28) - 6$. Add: $-42-6$. Subtract: $-48$.

{{< fillin
  question="Simplify: $8(-3) + 5(-7) - 4$"
  answer="-63"
  hint="Multiply first ($8 \cdot -3$ and $5 \cdot -7$), then add and subtract left to right."
>}}

**Example.** Simplify (a) $(-2)^4$, (b) $-2^4$.

(a) The exponent is $4$ and the base is $-2$: raise $-2$ to the fourth
power. In expanded form, $(-2)(-2)(-2)(-2) = 16$.
(b) The exponent is $4$ and the base is $2$: raise $2$ to the fourth
power, then take the opposite. In expanded form,
$-(2 \cdot 2 \cdot 2 \cdot 2) = -16$.

{{< fillin
  question="Simplify: $(-3)^4$"
  answer="81"
  hint="The base is $-3$ and the exponent is $4$ — multiply four factors of $-3$."
>}}

{{< fillin
  question="Simplify: $-3^4$"
  answer="-81"
  hint="The base is $3$ (not $-3$) — raise $3$ to the fourth power, then take the opposite."
>}}

**Example.** Simplify $12 - 3(9-12)$. Simplify inside the parentheses
first: $12-3(-3)$. Multiply: $12-(-9)$. Subtract: $21$.

{{< fillin
  question="Simplify: $17 - 4(8 - 11)$"
  answer="29"
  hint="Simplify inside the parentheses first ($8 - 11 = -3$), then multiply, then subtract."
>}}

**Example.** Simplify $8(-9) \div (-2)^3$. Simplify the exponent first:
$8(-9) \div (-8)$. Multiply: $-72 \div (-8)$. Divide: $9$.

{{< fillin
  question="Simplify: $12(-9) \div (-3)^3$"
  answer="4"
  hint="Simplify the exponent first ($(-3)^3 = -27$), then multiply, then divide."
>}}

**Example.** Simplify $-30 \div 2 + (-3)(-7)$. Divide first (left to
right with multiplication): $-15 + (-3)(-7)$. Multiply: $-15+21$. Add:
$6$.

{{< fillin
  question="Simplify: $-27 \div 3 + (-5)(-6)$"
  answer="21"
  hint="Divide and multiply first, left to right, then add."
>}}

## Evaluate variable expressions with integers

Now we can evaluate expressions that include multiplication and division
with integers — substitute the given numbers for the variables, then
simplify.

**Example.** Evaluate $2x^2 - 3x + 8$ when $x = -4$. Substitute $-4$ for
$x$: $2(-4)^2 - 3(-4) + 8$. Simplify the exponent: $2(16) - 3(-4) + 8$.
Multiply: $32 - (-12) + 8$. Subtract: $44 + 8$. Add: $52$. (Keep in mind
that we use parentheses to show the multiplication when substituting a
negative value — without them, $2 \cdot -4^2 - 3 \cdot -4 + 8$ would be
ambiguous.)

{{< fillin
  question="Evaluate: $3x^2 - 2x + 6$ when $x = -3$"
  answer="39"
  hint="Substitute $-3$ for $x$, using parentheses: $3(-3)^2 - 2(-3) + 6$."
>}}

**Example.** Evaluate $3x + 4y - 6$ when $x = -1$ and $y = 2$.
Substitute: $3(-1) + 4(2) - 6$. Multiply: $-3 + 8 - 6$. Simplify: $-1$.

{{< fillin
  question="Evaluate: $7x + 6y - 12$ when $x = -2$ and $y = 3$"
  answer="-8"
  hint="Substitute $-2$ for $x$ and $3$ for $y$, then simplify: $7(-2) + 6(3) - 12$."
>}}

## Translate word phrases to algebraic expressions

All our prior work translating words to algebra transfers to phrases
that include multiplying and dividing integers. The key word for
multiplication is *product*, and for division is *quotient*.

**Example.** Translate to an algebraic expression and simplify if
possible: the product of $-2$ and $14$. Translate: $(-2)(14)$. Simplify:
$-28$.

{{< fillin
  question="Translate to an algebraic expression and simplify if possible: the product of $-5$ and $12$"
  answer="-60"
  hint="'Product' means multiply: $(-5)(12)$."
>}}

**Example.** Translate to an algebraic expression and simplify if
possible: the quotient of $-56$ and $-7$. Translate: $-56 \div (-7)$.
Simplify: $8$.

{{< fillin
  question="Translate to an algebraic expression and simplify if possible: the quotient of $-63$ and $-9$"
  answer="7"
  hint="'Quotient' means divide: $-63 \div (-9)$."
>}}

## Key terms

**multiplication/division sign rule** — for both multiplying and
dividing signed numbers: same signs give a positive result; different
signs give a negative result. **multiplication and division by $-1$** —
multiplying or dividing a number by $-1$ gives its opposite.
