The whole numbers you've worked with so far can't describe everything —
a temperature below zero, a yard line lost on a football play, or an
overdrawn checking account all need numbers *less than zero*. This chapter
extends the number line in the negative direction and shows how addition,
subtraction, multiplication, and division work once negative numbers are
allowed.

## Sections

- **Introduction to Integers** — locating positive and negative numbers on
  the number line, ordering integers, opposites, and absolute value.
- **Add Integers** — modeling addition of integers with counters, and the
  rules for adding two integers with the same or different signs.
- **Subtract Integers** — modeling subtraction of integers with counters,
  and the rules for subtracting integers.
- **Multiply and Divide Integers** — the sign rules for multiplication and
  division, and simplifying expressions that combine integers with the
  order of operations.
- **Solve Equations Using Integers; The Division Property of Equality** —
  checking whether a number is a solution of an equation, and solving
  equations with negative numbers using the addition, subtraction, and
  division properties of equality.
