## Use the definition of percent

How many cents are in one dollar? There are $100$ cents in a dollar. How
many years are in a century? There are $100$ years in a century. Does this
give you a clue about what the word "percent" means? It is really two
words, "per cent," and means *per one hundred*. A **percent** is a ratio
whose denominator is $100$. We use the percent symbol, %, to show percent.

{{< callout type="info" >}}
  **Percent.** A percent is a ratio whose denominator is $100$.
{{< /callout >}}

According to data from the American Association of Community Colleges,
about $57\%$ of community college students are female. This means $57$ out
of every $100$ community college students are female. Out of $100$ equal
squares in a grid, $57$ would be shaded, which we write as the ratio
$\tfrac{57}{100}$.

Similarly, $25\%$ means a ratio of $\tfrac{25}{100}$, $3\%$ means a ratio of
$\tfrac{3}{100}$, and $100\%$ means a ratio of $\tfrac{100}{100}$. In words,
"one hundred percent" means the total $100\%$ is $\tfrac{100}{100}$, and
since $\tfrac{100}{100} = 1$, we see that $100\%$ means one whole.

**Example.** According to the Public Policy Institute of California, $44\%$
of parents of public school children would like their youngest child to
earn a graduate degree. Write this percent as a ratio.

The amount we want to convert is $44\%$. Since percent means per $100$,
$44\% = \tfrac{44}{100}$.

## Convert percents to fractions and decimals

Since percents are ratios, they can easily be expressed as fractions.
Remember that percent means per $100$, so the denominator of the fraction
is $100$.

{{< callout type="info" >}}
  **Convert a percent to a fraction.**

  1. Write the percent as a ratio with the denominator $100$.
  2. Simplify the fraction if possible.
{{< /callout >}}

**Example.** Convert each percent to a fraction: (a) $36\%$ (b) $125\%$.

(a) Write $36\%$ as a ratio with denominator $100$: $\tfrac{36}{100}$.
Simplify: $\tfrac{9}{25}$.

(b) Write $125\%$ as a ratio with denominator $100$: $\tfrac{125}{100}$.
Simplify: $\tfrac{5}{4}$.

This example shows that a percent can be greater than $1$ — $125\%$ means
$\tfrac{125}{100}$, or $\tfrac{5}{4}$. This is an improper fraction, and its
value is greater than one.

{{< fillin
  question="Convert each percent to a fraction, simplified: 48%"
  answer="\frac{12}{25}"
  answerDisplay="$\tfrac{12}{25}$"
  hint="Write $\tfrac{48}{100}$, then divide the numerator and denominator by their common factors."
>}}

{{< fillin
  question="Convert each percent to a fraction, simplified: 110%"
  answer="\frac{11}{10}"
  answerDisplay="$\tfrac{11}{10}$"
  hint="Write $\tfrac{110}{100}$, then simplify — the result is an improper fraction greater than 1."
>}}

**Example.** Convert each percent to a fraction: (a) $24.5\%$
(b) $33\tfrac{1}{3}\%$.

(a) Write $24.5\%$ as a ratio with denominator $100$: $\tfrac{24.5}{100}$.
Clear the decimal by multiplying the numerator and denominator by $10$:
$\tfrac{245}{1000}$. Rewrite showing common factors and simplify:
$\tfrac{5 \cdot 49}{5 \cdot 200} = \tfrac{49}{200}$.

(b) Write $33\tfrac{1}{3}\%$ as a ratio with denominator $100$:
$\tfrac{33\frac{1}{3}}{100}$. Write the numerator as an improper fraction:
$\tfrac{\frac{100}{3}}{100}$. Rewrite as fraction division, replacing $100$
with $\tfrac{100}{1}$: $\tfrac{100}{3} \div \tfrac{100}{1}$. Multiply by the
reciprocal: $\tfrac{100}{3} \cdot \tfrac{1}{100}$. Simplify: $\tfrac{1}{3}$.

In Decimals, we learned how to convert fractions to decimals. To convert a
percent to a decimal, we first convert it to a fraction and then change the
fraction to a decimal.

{{< callout type="info" >}}
  **Convert a percent to a decimal.**

  1. Write the percent as a ratio with the denominator $100$.
  2. Convert the fraction to a decimal by dividing the numerator by the
     denominator.
{{< /callout >}}

**Example.** Convert each percent to a decimal: (a) $6\%$ (b) $78\%$.

Because we want to change to a decimal, we leave the fractions with
denominator $100$ instead of removing common factors.

(a) Write $6\%$ as $\tfrac{6}{100}$. Divide $6$ by $100$: $0.06$.

(b) Write $78\%$ as $\tfrac{78}{100}$. Divide $78$ by $100$: $0.78$.

  answer="0.03"
  hint="Write $\tfrac{3}{100}$, then divide 3 by 100."
>}}

{{< fillin
  question="Convert each percent to a decimal: 91%"

>}}

**Example.** Convert each percent to a decimal: (a) $135\%$
(b) $12.5\%$.

(a) Write $135\%$ as $\tfrac{135}{100}$. Divide $135$ by $100$: $1.35$.

(b) Write $12.5\%$ as $\tfrac{12.5}{100}$. Divide $12.5$ by $100$: $0.125$.

Let's summarize the results so we can look for a pattern.

| Percent | Decimal |
| :---: | :---: |
| $6\%$ | $0.06$ |
| $78\%$ | $0.78$ |
| $135\%$ | $1.35$ |
| $12.5\%$ | $0.125$ |

Do you see the pattern? To convert a percent number to a decimal number, we
move the decimal point two places to the left and remove the % sign.
(Sometimes the decimal point does not appear in the percent number, but
just like we can think of the integer $6$ as $6.0$, we can think of $6\%$
as $6.0\%$.) Notice that we may need to add zeros in front of the number
when moving the decimal to the left.

## Convert decimals and fractions to percents

To convert a decimal to a percent, remember that percent means per hundred.
If we change the decimal to a fraction whose denominator is $100$, it is
easy to change that fraction to a percent.

{{< callout type="info" >}}
  **Convert a decimal to a percent.**

  1. Write the decimal as a fraction.
  2. If the denominator of the fraction is not $100$, rewrite it as an
     equivalent fraction with denominator $100$.
  3. Write this ratio as a percent.
{{< /callout >}}

**Example.** Convert each decimal to a percent: (a) $0.05$ (b) $0.83$.

(a) Write $0.05$ as a fraction: $\tfrac{5}{100}$. Write this ratio as a
percent: $5\%$.

(b) Write $0.83$ as a fraction: $\tfrac{83}{100}$. Write this ratio as a
percent: $83\%$.

{{< fillin
  question="Convert each decimal to a percent: 0.01"
  answer="1"
  answerDisplay="1%"
  hint="The denominator is already 100 — write the numerator with a % sign."
>}}

{{< fillin
  question="Convert each decimal to a percent: 0.17"
  answer="17"
  answerDisplay="17%"
  hint="The denominator is already 100 — write the numerator with a % sign."
>}}

To convert a mixed number or a decimal greater than $1$ to a percent, we
follow the same steps.

**Example.** Convert each decimal to a percent: (a) $1.05$ (b) $0.075$.

(a) Write $1.05$ as a fraction: $1\tfrac{5}{100}$. Write as an improper
fraction with denominator $100$: $\tfrac{105}{100}$. Write this ratio as a
percent: $105\%$. Notice that since $1.05 > 1$, the result is more than
$100\%$.

(b) Write $0.075$ as a fraction: $\tfrac{75}{1{,}000}$. Divide the
numerator and denominator by $10$, so that the denominator is $100$:
$\tfrac{7.5}{100}$. Write this ratio as a percent: $7.5\%$.

Do you see the pattern? To convert a decimal to a percent, we move the
decimal point two places to the right and then add the percent sign.

In Decimals, we also learned how to convert fractions to decimals. So to
convert a fraction to a percent, we first change it to a decimal and then
convert that decimal to a percent.

{{< callout type="info" >}}
  **Convert a fraction to a percent.**

  1. Convert the fraction to a decimal.
  2. Convert the decimal to a percent.
{{< /callout >}}

**Example.** Convert each fraction or mixed number to a percent:
(a) $\tfrac{3}{4}$ (b) $\tfrac{11}{8}$ (c) $2\tfrac{1}{5}$.

To convert a fraction to a decimal, divide the numerator by the
denominator.

(a) Change $\tfrac{3}{4}$ to a decimal: $0.75$. Write as a percent by
moving the decimal two places: $75\%$.

(b) Change $\tfrac{11}{8}$ to a decimal: $1.375$. Write as a percent by
moving the decimal two places: $137.5\%$.

(c) Write $2\tfrac{1}{5}$ as an improper fraction: $\tfrac{11}{5}$. Change
to a decimal: $2.2$. Write as a percent: $220\%$.

Notice that we needed to add zeros at the end of the number when moving
the decimal two places to the right.

Sometimes when changing a fraction to a decimal, the division continues
for many decimal places and we round off the quotient. The number of
decimal places we round to will depend on the situation. If the decimal
involves money, we round to the hundredths place. For most other cases we
round the number to the nearest thousandth, so the percent will be rounded
to the nearest tenth.

**Example.** Convert $\tfrac{5}{7}$ to a percent.

Change $\tfrac{5}{7}$ to a decimal, rounding to the nearest thousandth:
$0.714$. Write as a percent: $71.4\%$.

When we first looked at fractions and decimals, we saw that some fractions
convert to a repeating decimal. We use the same notation — a line over the
repeating digits — as well as fraction notation, when we convert fractions
to percents.

**Example.** An article in a medical journal claimed that approximately
$\tfrac{1}{3}$ of American adults are obese. Convert the fraction
$\tfrac{1}{3}$ to a percent.

Change $\tfrac{1}{3}$ to a decimal by dividing $1$ by $3$: $0.333\ldots$,
a repeating decimal. Write as a percent: $33\tfrac{1}{3}\%$. We could also
write the percent as $33.\overline{3}\%$.
