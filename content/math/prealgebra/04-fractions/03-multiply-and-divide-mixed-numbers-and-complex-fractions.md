## Multiply and divide mixed numbers

In the previous section, every example used proper or improper fractions.
What happens when we're asked to multiply or divide mixed numbers?
Remember that we can convert a mixed number to an improper fraction — so
that's exactly what we do first.

{{< callout type="info" >}}
  **Multiply or divide mixed numbers.**

  1. Convert the mixed numbers to improper fractions.
  2. Follow the rules for fraction multiplication or division.
  3. Simplify if possible.
{{< /callout >}}

**Example.** Multiply: $3\tfrac{1}{3} \cdot \tfrac{5}{8}$.

Convert $3\tfrac{1}{3}$ to the improper fraction $\tfrac{10}{3}$. Multiply:
$\tfrac{10}{3} \cdot \tfrac{5}{8} = \tfrac{10 \cdot 5}{3 \cdot 8}$. Look
for common factors — $2$ divides both $10$ and $8$ — and remove them:

$$3\frac{1}{3} \cdot \frac{5}{8} = \frac{25}{12}$$

We leave the answer as an improper fraction rather than converting it to
a mixed number. In algebra, it's preferable to write answers as improper
fractions instead of mixed numbers — this avoids any possible confusion
between $2\tfrac{1}{12}$ (a mixed number) and $2 \cdot \tfrac{1}{12}$ (a
product).

**Example.** Multiply, and write the answer in simplified form:
$2\tfrac{4}{5}\left(-1\tfrac{7}{8}\right)$.

Convert both mixed numbers to improper fractions: $\tfrac{14}{5}$ and
$-\tfrac{15}{8}$. Multiply — the product is negative — and remove the
common factors of $5$ and $2$:

$$2\frac{4}{5}\left(-1\frac{7}{8}\right) = -\frac{21}{4}$$

**Example.** Divide, and write the answer in simplified form:
$3\tfrac{4}{7} \div 5$.

Convert $3\tfrac{4}{7}$ to $\tfrac{25}{7}$, and write $5$ as
$\tfrac{5}{1}$. Multiply by the reciprocal:
$\tfrac{25}{7} \cdot \tfrac{1}{5}$. Removing the common factor of $5$:

$$3\frac{4}{7} \div 5 = \frac{5}{7}$$

{{< fillin
  question="Divide, and write your answer in simplified form: $2\tfrac{5}{8} \div 3$"

**Example.** Divide: $2\tfrac{1}{2} \div 1\tfrac{1}{4}$.

Convert both to improper fractions: $\tfrac{5}{2} \div \tfrac{5}{4}$.
Multiply by the reciprocal of the second: $\tfrac{5}{2} \cdot
\tfrac{4}{5}$. Removing common factors of $5$ and $2$ gives:

$$2\frac{1}{2} \div 1\frac{1}{4} = 2$$

## Translate phrases to expressions with fractions

The words *quotient* and *ratio* are often used to describe fractions. The
**quotient** of $a$ and $b$ is the result of dividing $a$ by $b$, or
$\tfrac{a}{b}$.

**Example.** Translate the phrase into an algebraic expression: "the
quotient of $3x$ and $8$."

The keyword *quotient* tells us the operation is division. The words *of*
and *and* mark the two numbers to divide: we need to divide $3x$ by $8$:

$$\frac{3x}{8}$$

{{< fillin
  question="Translate the phrase into an algebraic expression: the quotient of $9s$ and $14$"
  answer="\frac{9s}{14}"
  answerDisplay="$\tfrac{9s}{14}$"
  hint="Quotient means division — the first named quantity goes on top."
>}}

**Example.** Translate the phrase into an algebraic expression: "the
quotient of the difference of $m$ and $n$, and $p$."

We want the quotient of the *difference* of $m$ and $n$, and $p$ — that
is, we divide the difference of $m$ and $n$ by $p$:

$$\frac{m-n}{p}$$

{{< fillin
  question="Translate the phrase into an algebraic expression: the quotient of the sum of $p$ and $q$, and $r$"
  answer="\frac{p+q}{r}"
  answerDisplay="$\tfrac{p+q}{r}$"
  hint="First find the sum named in the phrase, then divide by the last quantity."
>}}

## Simplify complex fractions

Our work with fractions so far has included proper fractions, improper
fractions, and mixed numbers. Another kind of fraction is called a
**complex fraction** — a fraction in which the numerator or the
denominator contains a fraction. Some examples of complex fractions are

$$\frac{\tfrac{6}{7}}{3}, \qquad \frac{\tfrac{3}{4}}{\tfrac{5}{8}}, \qquad \frac{\tfrac{x}{2}}{\tfrac{xy}{6}}$$

To simplify a complex fraction, remember that the fraction bar means
division. So the complex fraction

$$\cfrac{\tfrac{3}{4}}{\tfrac{5}{8}}$$

can be written as $\tfrac{3}{4} \div \tfrac{5}{8}$.

{{< callout type="info" >}}
  **Simplify a complex fraction.**

  1. Rewrite the complex fraction as a division problem.
  2. Follow the rules for dividing fractions.
  3. Simplify if possible.
{{< /callout >}}

**Example.** Simplify:

$$\cfrac{\tfrac{3}{4}}{\tfrac{5}{8}}$$

Rewrite as division: $\tfrac{3}{4} \div \tfrac{5}{8}$. Multiply by the
reciprocal of the second fraction: $\tfrac{3}{4} \cdot \tfrac{8}{5}$.
Removing the common factor of $4$:

$$\frac{\tfrac{3}{4}}{\tfrac{5}{8}} = \frac{6}{5}$$

{{< fillin
  question="Simplify: $\left(\tfrac{2}{3}\right) / \left(\tfrac{5}{6}\right)$"
  answer="\frac{4}{5}"
  answerDisplay="$\tfrac{4}{5}$"
  hint="Rewrite the complex fraction as $\tfrac{2}{3} \div \tfrac{5}{6}$, then multiply by the reciprocal of the second fraction."
>}}

**Example.** Simplify:

$$\cfrac{-\tfrac{6}{7}}{3}$$

Rewrite as division: $-\tfrac{6}{7} \div 3$. Write $3$ as $\tfrac{3}{1}$,
multiply by its reciprocal, $-\tfrac{6}{7} \cdot \tfrac{1}{3}$, and remove
the common factor of $3$:

$$\frac{-\tfrac{6}{7}}{3} = -\frac{2}{7}$$

{{< fillin
  question="Simplify: $\left(-\tfrac{8}{7}\right) / 4$"
  answer="-\frac{2}{7}"
  answerDisplay="$-\tfrac{2}{7}$"
  hint="Rewrite as $-\tfrac{8}{7} \div 4$, write $4$ as $\tfrac{4}{1}$, then multiply by its reciprocal and simplify."
>}}

**Example.** Simplify:

$$\cfrac{\tfrac{x}{2}}{\tfrac{xy}{6}}$$

Rewrite as division: $\tfrac{x}{2} \div \tfrac{xy}{6}$. Multiply by the
reciprocal: $\tfrac{x}{2} \cdot \tfrac{6}{xy}$. Removing the common
factors of $x$ and $2$:

$$\frac{\tfrac{x}{2}}{\tfrac{xy}{6}} = \frac{3}{y}$$

{{< fillin
  question="Simplify: $\left(\tfrac{p}{2}\right) / \left(\tfrac{pq}{8}\right)$. Answer in terms of $q$."
  answer="\frac{4}{q}"
  answerDisplay="$\tfrac{4}{q}$"
  hint="Rewrite as $\tfrac{p}{2} \div \tfrac{pq}{8}$, multiply by the reciprocal, then remove the common factor of $p$."
>}}

**Example.** Simplify:

$$\cfrac{2\tfrac{3}{4}}{\tfrac{1}{8}}$$

Rewrite as division: $2\tfrac{3}{4} \div \tfrac{1}{8}$. Change the mixed
number to an improper fraction: $\tfrac{11}{4} \div \tfrac{1}{8}$.
Multiply by the reciprocal: $\tfrac{11}{4} \cdot \tfrac{8}{1}$. Removing
the common factor of $4$:

$$\frac{2\tfrac{3}{4}}{\tfrac{1}{8}} = 22$$

  answer="\frac{5}{6}"
  answerDisplay="$\tfrac{5}{6}$"
  hint="Convert $3\tfrac{1}{5}$ to the improper fraction $\tfrac{16}{5}$, rewrite as division, then multiply by the reciprocal."
>}}

## Simplify expressions with a fraction bar

Where does the negative sign go in a fraction? Usually it's placed in
front of the whole fraction, but you'll sometimes see a fraction with a
negative numerator or denominator. Remember that a fraction represents
division: $-\tfrac{1}{3}$ could be the result of dividing $\tfrac{-1}{3}$
(a negative by a positive), or of dividing $\tfrac{1}{-3}$ (a positive by
a negative) — either way, a negative divided by a positive, or a positive
divided by a negative, gives a negative quotient. If *both* the numerator
and denominator are negative, the fraction is positive, since a negative
divided by a negative is positive.

{{< callout type="info" >}}
  **Placement of negative sign in a fraction.** For any positive numbers
  $a$ and $b$,

  $$\frac{-a}{b} = \frac{a}{-b} = -\frac{a}{b}$$
{{< /callout >}}

**Example.** Which of the following fractions are equivalent to
$\tfrac{7}{-8}$? $\quad \tfrac{-7}{-8}, \quad \tfrac{-7}{8}, \quad
\tfrac{7}{8}, \quad -\tfrac{7}{8}$

The quotient of a positive and a negative is negative, so $\tfrac{7}{-8}$
is negative. Of the listed fractions, $\tfrac{-7}{8}$ and $-\tfrac{7}{8}$
are also negative, so those are equivalent to $\tfrac{7}{-8}$.

{{< fillin
  question="Is $-\tfrac{3}{5}$ equivalent to $\tfrac{3}{-5}$? Enter 1 for yes or 0 for no."
  answer="1"
  hint="A negative divided by a positive equals a positive divided by a negative — both give the same negative value."
>}}

Fraction bars act as grouping symbols — the expressions above and below
the bar should be treated as if they were in parentheses. For example,
$\tfrac{4+8}{5-3}$ means $(4+8) \div (5-3)$: the order of operations tells
us to simplify the numerator and the denominator first, as if each were
in its own parentheses, before dividing.

{{< callout type="info" >}}
  **Simplify an expression with a fraction bar.**

  1. Simplify the numerator.
  2. Simplify the denominator.
  3. Simplify the fraction.
{{< /callout >}}

**Example.** Simplify: $\tfrac{4+8}{5-3}$.

Simplify the numerator: $4 + 8 = 12$. Simplify the denominator:
$5 - 3 = 2$. Simplify the fraction:

$$\frac{4+8}{5-3} = 6$$

{{< fillin
  question="Simplify: $\tfrac{4+6}{11-2}$"
  answer="\frac{10}{9}"
  answerDisplay="$\tfrac{10}{9}$"
  hint="Simplify the numerator and denominator separately first, then divide."
>}}

**Example.** Simplify: $\tfrac{4-2(3)}{2^2+2}$.

Use the order of operations in the numerator (multiply first) and in the
denominator (apply the exponent first): $\tfrac{4-6}{4+2}$. Simplify the
numerator and denominator: $\tfrac{-2}{6}$. Simplify the fraction:

$$\frac{4-2(3)}{2^2+2} = -\frac{1}{3}$$

{{< fillin
  question="Simplify: $\tfrac{6-3(5)}{3^2+3}$"
  answer="-\frac{3}{4}"
  answerDisplay="$-\tfrac{3}{4}$"
  hint="In the numerator, multiply before subtracting. In the denominator, apply the exponent before adding."
>}}

**Example.** Simplify: $\tfrac{(8-4)^2}{8^2-4^2}$.

Use the order of operations — parentheses first, then exponents:
$\tfrac{4^2}{64-16} = \tfrac{16}{48}$. Simplify the fraction:

$$\frac{(8-4)^2}{8^2-4^2} = \frac{1}{3}$$

{{< fillin
  question="Simplify: $(11-7)^2 / (11^2-7^2)$"
  answer="\frac{2}{9}"
  answerDisplay="$\tfrac{2}{9}$"
  hint="Simplify inside the parentheses first, then apply exponents in both the numerator and denominator."
>}}

**Example.** Simplify: $\tfrac{4(-3)+6(-2)}{-3(2)-2}$.

Multiply in the numerator and denominator: $\tfrac{-12+(-12)}{-6-2}$.
Simplify: $\tfrac{-24}{-8}$. Divide:

$$\frac{4(-3)+6(-2)}{-3(2)-2} = 3$$

  question="Simplify: $\tfrac{8(-2)+4(-3)}{-5(2)+3}$"
  answer="4"
  hint="Multiply out the numerator and the denominator separately before dividing."
>}}

## Key terms

**complex fraction** — a fraction in which the numerator, the
denominator, or both, contain a fraction. **fraction bar as grouping
symbol** — the numerator and denominator of a fraction are each treated
as if enclosed in parentheses, so each is simplified fully before
dividing.
