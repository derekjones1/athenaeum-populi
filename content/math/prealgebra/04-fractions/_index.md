Often in life, whole amounts are not exactly what we need. A baker must use
a little more than a cup of milk or part of a teaspoon of sugar; a carpenter
might need less than a foot of wood. In this chapter, we work with numbers
that describe parts of a whole — fractions — and build the skills to model,
compare, and compute with them.

## Sections

- **Visualize Fractions** — the meaning of fractions, modeling improper
  fractions and mixed numbers, converting between them, equivalent
  fractions, and locating and ordering fractions on the number line.
- **Multiply and Divide Fractions** — simplifying fractions, multiplying
  and dividing fractions, and simplifying expressions with a fraction bar.
- **Multiply and Divide Mixed Numbers and Complex Fractions** — multiplying
  and dividing mixed numbers, translating phrases into expressions, and
  simplifying complex fractions.
- **Add and Subtract Fractions with Common Denominators** — modeling and
  computing sums and differences of fractions that already share a
  denominator.
- **Add and Subtract Fractions with Different Denominators** — finding the
  least common denominator and adding or subtracting fractions after
  converting to it.
- **Add and Subtract Mixed Numbers** — adding and subtracting mixed numbers
  with common and different denominators.
- **Solve Equations with Fractions** — using the properties of equality,
  and the multiplication property of equality, to solve equations that
  contain fractions.
