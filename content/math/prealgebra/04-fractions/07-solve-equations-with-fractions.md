## Determine whether a fraction is a solution of an equation

A solution of an equation is a value that makes a true statement when
substituted for the variable. The steps for checking whether a number is
a solution are the same whether the solution is a whole number, an
integer, or a fraction.

{{< callout type="info" >}}
  **Determine whether a number is a solution to an equation.**

  1. Substitute the number for the variable in the equation.
  2. Simplify the expressions on both sides of the equation.
  3. Determine whether the resulting equation is true. If it is true, the
     number is a solution. If it is not true, the number is not a
     solution.
{{< /callout >}}

**Example.** Determine whether each of the following is a solution of
$x - \tfrac{3}{10} = \tfrac{1}{2}$: (a) $x=1$; (b) $x=\tfrac{4}{5}$; (c)
$x=-\tfrac{4}{5}$.

(a) Substitute $1$ for $x$: $1 - \tfrac{3}{10} \stackrel{?}{=} \tfrac{1}{2}$.
Rewriting with LCD $10$: $\tfrac{10}{10} - \tfrac{3}{10} = \tfrac{7}{10}$,
which is not equal to $\tfrac{5}{10}$. So $x=1$ is *not* a solution.

(b) Substitute $\tfrac{4}{5}$ for $x$:
$\tfrac{4}{5} - \tfrac{3}{10} \stackrel{?}{=} \tfrac{1}{2}$. Rewriting:
$\tfrac{8}{10} - \tfrac{3}{10} = \tfrac{5}{10} = \tfrac{5}{10}$. True, so
$x=\tfrac{4}{5}$ *is* a solution.

(c) Substitute $-\tfrac{4}{5}$ for $x$:
$-\tfrac{8}{10} - \tfrac{3}{10} = -\tfrac{11}{10}$, which is not equal to
$\tfrac{5}{10}$. So $x=-\tfrac{4}{5}$ is *not* a solution.

## Solve equations with fractions using the Addition, Subtraction, and Division Properties of Equality

We use the same properties of equality with fractions as we did with
whole numbers and integers.

{{< callout type="info" >}}
  **Addition, Subtraction, and Division Properties of Equality.** For any
  numbers $a$, $b$, and $c$: if $a=b$, then $a+c=b+c$ (Addition Property);
  if $a=b$, then $a-c=b-c$ (Subtraction Property); if $a=b$, then
  $\tfrac{a}{c}=\tfrac{b}{c}$, $c \neq 0$ (Division Property).

  When you add, subtract, or divide both sides of an equation by the
  same quantity, you still have equality.
{{< /callout >}}

**Example.** Solve: $y + \tfrac{9}{16} = \tfrac{5}{16}$.

Subtract $\tfrac{9}{16}$ from each side to undo the addition:
$y = \tfrac{5}{16} - \tfrac{9}{16} = -\tfrac{4}{16}$. Simplify:

$$y = -\frac{1}{4}$$

Checking by substituting $-\tfrac{1}{4}$ back in confirms the solution.

  hint="Subtract $\tfrac{11}{12}$ from both sides to isolate $y$."

**Example.** Solve: $a - \tfrac{5}{9} = -\tfrac{8}{9}$.

Add $\tfrac{5}{9}$ to each side to undo the subtraction:
$a = -\tfrac{8}{9} + \tfrac{5}{9} = -\tfrac{3}{9}$. Simplify:

$$a = -\frac{1}{3}$$

The next example may not look like it has a fraction, but see what
happens when we solve it.

**Example.** Solve: $10q = 44$.

Divide both sides by $10$ to undo the multiplication: $q = \tfrac{44}{10}$.
Simplify:

$$q = \frac{22}{5}$$

The solution is a fraction — we leave it in that form rather than
converting to a mixed number, since it came from solving an equation.

## Solve equations using the Multiplication Property of Equality

Consider the equation $\tfrac{x}{4} = 3$. To "undo" the division, we
multiply both sides by $4$ — the **Multiplication Property of Equality**.

{{< callout type="info" >}}
  **Multiplication Property of Equality.** For any numbers $a$, $b$, and
  $c$: if $a=b$, then $ac=bc$.

  If you multiply both sides of an equation by the same quantity, you
  still have equality.
{{< /callout >}}

**Example.** Solve: $\tfrac{x}{7} = -9$.

Multiply both sides by $7$ to isolate $x$: $7 \cdot \tfrac{x}{7} = 7(-9)$.
Simplify:

$$x = -63$$

{{< fillin
  question="Solve: $\tfrac{f}{5} = -25$"
  answer="-125"
  hint="Multiply both sides by $5$ to isolate $f$."
>}}

**Example.** Solve: $\tfrac{p}{-8} = -40$.

Here, $p$ is divided by $-8$; multiply both sides by $-8$ to isolate
$p$: $-8 \cdot \tfrac{p}{-8} = -8(-40)$. Simplify:

$$p = 320$$

### Solve equations with a coefficient of $-1$

Look at the equation $-y = 15$. It might look like $y$ is already
isolated, but there's a negative sign in front, so it's not. There are
three ways to isolate the variable here: rewrite $-y$ as $-1y$ and divide
both sides by $-1$; multiply both sides by $-1$; or read $-y$ as "the
opposite of $y$" and ask what number has $15$ as its opposite.

**Example.** Solve: $-y = 15$.

Rewriting $-y$ as $-1y$ and dividing both sides by $-1$:
$\tfrac{-1y}{-1} = \tfrac{15}{-1}$, so $y = -15$. (Multiplying both sides
by $-1$, or reasoning that the opposite of $-15$ is $15$, gives the same
answer.)

### Solve equations with a fraction coefficient

When an equation has a fraction coefficient, we use the Multiplication
Property of Equality to make the coefficient equal to $1$ — by
multiplying both sides by the reciprocal of the coefficient.

**Example.** Solve: $\tfrac{3}{4}x = 24$.

The coefficient of $x$ is $\tfrac{3}{4}$. Multiply both sides by its
reciprocal, $\tfrac{4}{3}$: $\tfrac{4}{3} \cdot \tfrac{3}{4}x =
\tfrac{4}{3} \cdot 24$. Simplify:

$$x = 32$$

Notice we could instead have divided both sides by $\tfrac{3}{4}$ — the
same result, since dividing by a fraction is the same as multiplying by
its reciprocal — but multiplying by the reciprocal is usually easier.

{{< fillin
  question="Solve: $\tfrac{2}{5} n = 14$"
  answer="35"
  hint="Multiply both sides by the reciprocal of $\tfrac{2}{5}$, which is $\tfrac{5}{2}$."
>}}

**Example.** Solve: $-\tfrac{3}{8}w = 72$.

The coefficient is a negative fraction, so its reciprocal is also
negative. Multiply both sides by $-\tfrac{8}{3}$:
$-\tfrac{8}{3}\left(-\tfrac{3}{8}w\right) = -\tfrac{8}{3}(72)$. Simplify:

$$w = -192$$

## Translate sentences to equations and solve

Now we've covered all four properties of equality — addition,
subtraction, multiplication, and division. When you add, subtract,
multiply, or divide the same quantity on both sides of an equation, you
still have equality.

**Example.** Translate and solve: "$n$ divided by $6$ is $-24$."

Translate: $\tfrac{n}{6} = -24$. Multiply both sides by $6$:
$n = 6(-24)$. Simplify:

$$n = -144$$

Checking: is $-144$ divided by $6$ equal to $-24$? Yes.

{{< fillin
  question="Translate and solve: $n$ divided by 7 is equal to -21."
  answer="-147"
  hint="Translate to $\tfrac{n}{7} = -21$, then multiply both sides by $7$."
>}}

**Example.** Translate and solve: "The quotient of $q$ and $-5$ is $70$."

Translate: $\tfrac{q}{-5} = 70$. Multiply both sides by $-5$:
$q = -5(70)$. Simplify:

$$q = -350$$

{{< fillin
  question="Translate and solve: The quotient of $q$ and -8 is 72."
  answer="-576"
  hint="Translate to $\tfrac{q}{-8} = 72$, then multiply both sides by $-8$."
>}}

**Example.** Translate and solve: "Two-thirds of $f$ is $18$."

Translate: $\tfrac{2}{3}f = 18$. Multiply both sides by the reciprocal,
$\tfrac{3}{2}$: $f = \tfrac{3}{2} \cdot 18$. Simplify:

$$f = 27$$

{{< fillin
  question="Translate and solve: Two-fifths of $f$ is 16."
  answer="40"
  hint="Translate to $\tfrac{2}{5}f = 16$, then multiply both sides by the reciprocal, $\tfrac{5}{2}$."
>}}

**Example.** Translate and solve: "The quotient of $m$ and $\tfrac{5}{6}$
is $\tfrac{3}{4}$."

Translate: $\tfrac{m}{\tfrac{5}{6}} = \tfrac{3}{4}$. Multiply both sides
by $\tfrac{5}{6}$ to isolate $m$: $m = \tfrac{5}{6} \cdot \tfrac{3}{4}$.
Removing common factors:

$$m = \frac{5}{8}$$

  question="Translate and solve: The quotient of $n$ and $\tfrac{2}{3}$ is $\tfrac{5}{12}$."
  answer="\frac{5}{18}"
  answerDisplay="$\tfrac{5}{18}$"
  hint="Translate to $n / \left(\tfrac{2}{3}\right) = \tfrac{5}{12}$, then multiply both sides by $\tfrac{2}{3}$."
>}}

**Example.** Translate and solve: "The sum of three-eighths and $x$ is
three and one-half."

Translate: $\tfrac{3}{8} + x = 3\tfrac{1}{2}$. Subtract $\tfrac{3}{8}$
from both sides: $x = 3\tfrac{1}{2} - \tfrac{3}{8}$. Convert
$3\tfrac{1}{2}$ to the improper fraction $\tfrac{7}{2}$, then to LCD $8$:
$\tfrac{28}{8} - \tfrac{3}{8} = \tfrac{25}{8}$. Write as a mixed number:

$$x = 3\frac{1}{8}$$

We write the answer as a mixed number since the original problem used a
mixed number.

## Key terms

**solution of an equation** — a value that, substituted for the
variable, makes the equation a true statement. **Multiplication Property
of Equality** — for any numbers $a$, $b$, $c$: if $a=b$, then $ac=bc$.
