## Simplify fractions

In working with equivalent fractions, we saw that there are many ways to
write a fraction that represents the same value. How do we know which one
to use? Often we use the fraction that is in **simplified form**.

{{< callout type="info" >}}
  **Simplified fraction.** A fraction is considered simplified if there
  are no common factors, other than $1$, in the numerator and
  denominator.
{{< /callout >}}

For example, $\tfrac{2}{3}$ is simplified because $2$ and $3$ have no
common factors, but $\tfrac{10}{15}$ is not simplified because $5$ is a
common factor of $10$ and $15$.

Simplifying a fraction is often called *reducing* it. We use the
Equivalent Fractions Property in reverse to simplify: rewriting both
forms together,

$$\frac{a \cdot c}{b \cdot c} = \frac{a}{b} \qquad (b \neq 0,\ c \neq 0)$$

Since $c$ is a common factor of the numerator and denominator, it can be
removed.

{{< callout type="info" >}}
  **Simplify a fraction.**

  1. Rewrite the numerator and denominator to show their common factors.
     If needed, factor the numerator and denominator into primes first.
  2. Simplify, using the Equivalent Fractions Property, by removing common
     factors.
  3. Multiply any remaining factors.
{{< /callout >}}

**Example.** Simplify $\tfrac{10}{15}$.

Notice $5$ is a factor of both $10$ and $15$. Factor: $\tfrac{10}{15} =
\tfrac{2 \cdot 5}{3 \cdot 5}$. Remove the common factor of $5$:

$$\frac{10}{15} = \frac{2}{3}$$

{{< fillin
  question="Simplify: $\tfrac{8}{12}$"
  answer="\frac{2}{3}"
  answerDisplay="$\tfrac{2}{3}$"
  hint="Factor out the common factor of $4$ from both numerator and denominator."
>}}

To simplify a negative fraction, use the same process and keep the
negative sign.

**Example.** Simplify $-\tfrac{18}{24}$.

Both $18$ and $24$ have a factor of $6$: $-\tfrac{18}{24} =
-\tfrac{3 \cdot 6}{4 \cdot 6}$. Remove the common factor:

$$-\frac{18}{24} = -\frac{3}{4}$$

When we simplify an improper fraction, there is no need to convert it to
a mixed number first.

**Example.** Simplify $-\tfrac{56}{32}$.

Both $56$ and $32$ share a factor of $8$: $-\tfrac{56}{32} =
-\tfrac{7 \cdot 8}{4 \cdot 8}$. Removing the common factor gives
$-\tfrac{7}{4}$.

{{< fillin
  question="Simplify: $-\tfrac{54}{42}$"
  answer="-\frac{9}{7}"
  answerDisplay="$-\tfrac{9}{7}$"
  hint="Find the largest common factor of $54$ and $42$ (it's $6$), then remove it."
>}}

Sometimes it isn't easy to spot common factors of the numerator and
denominator. A good approach is to factor both into primes first — using
the factor tree method — then remove the factors that appear in both.

**Example.** Simplify $\tfrac{210}{385}$.

Factor tree the numerator and denominator into primes:
$210 = 2 \cdot 3 \cdot 5 \cdot 7$ and $385 = 5 \cdot 7 \cdot 11$. Rewrite:

$$\frac{210}{385} = \frac{2 \cdot 3 \cdot 5 \cdot 7}{5 \cdot 7 \cdot 11}$$

Remove the common factors of $5$ and $7$:

$$\frac{210}{385} = \frac{2 \cdot 3}{11} = \frac{6}{11}$$

{{< fillin
  question="Simplify: $\tfrac{120}{192}$. Factor each into primes first if it helps."
  answer="\frac{5}{8}"
  answerDisplay="$\tfrac{5}{8}$"
  hint="$120 = 2\cdot2\cdot2\cdot3\cdot5$ and $192 = 2\cdot2\cdot2\cdot2\cdot2\cdot2\cdot3$. Remove the common factors of $2$ (three of them) and $3$."
>}}

We can also simplify fractions containing variables the same way,
removing a common factor from numerator and denominator.

**Example.** Simplify $\tfrac{5xy}{15x}$.

Rewrite showing the common factors: $\tfrac{5xy}{15x} =
\tfrac{5 \cdot x \cdot y}{3 \cdot 5 \cdot x}$. Remove the common factors
of $5$ and $x$:

$$\frac{5xy}{15x} = \frac{y}{3}$$

  question="Simplify: $\tfrac{9a}{9b}$"
  answer="\frac{a}{b}"
  answerDisplay="$\tfrac{a}{b}$"
  hint="$9$ is a common factor of the numerator and denominator — remove it."
>}}

## Multiply fractions

A model can help you understand multiplying fractions. Think of
$\tfrac{1}{2} \cdot \tfrac{3}{4}$ as "one-half *of* three-fourths." If we
shade $\tfrac{3}{4}$ of a rectangle, then heavily shade half of that
shaded region, we find that $3$ out of $8$ pieces are heavily shaded — so
$\tfrac{1}{2} \cdot \tfrac{3}{4} = \tfrac{3}{8}$.

Do you notice we could have gotten the same answer by multiplying the
numerators together and the denominators together?
$\tfrac{1 \cdot 3}{2 \cdot 4} = \tfrac{3}{8}$.

{{< callout type="info" >}}
  **Fraction multiplication.** If $a$, $b$, $c$, and $d$ are numbers where
  $b \neq 0$ and $d \neq 0$, then

  $$\frac{a}{b} \cdot \frac{c}{d} = \frac{ac}{bd}$$

  To multiply fractions, multiply the numerators and multiply the
  denominators, then write the result in simplified form.
{{< /callout >}}

**Example.** Multiply, and write the answer in simplified form:
$\tfrac{3}{4} \cdot \tfrac{1}{5}$.

Multiply the numerators and denominators: $\tfrac{3 \cdot 1}{4 \cdot 5} =
\tfrac{3}{20}$. There are no common factors, so the fraction is already
simplified.

{{< fillin
  question="Multiply, and write the answer in simplified form: $\tfrac{1}{3} \cdot \tfrac{2}{5}$"
  answer="\frac{2}{15}"
  answerDisplay="$\tfrac{2}{15}$"
  hint="Multiply the numerators together and the denominators together."
>}}

When multiplying fractions, the properties of positive and negative
numbers still apply — determine the sign of the product first.

**Example.** Multiply, and write the answer in simplified form:
$-\tfrac{5}{8}\left(-\tfrac{2}{3}\right)$.

The signs are the same, so the product is positive. Multiplying:
$\tfrac{5 \cdot 2}{8 \cdot 3} = \tfrac{10}{24}$. Rewriting to show the
common factor of $2$ and removing it gives $\tfrac{5}{12}$.

It's often faster to remove common factors *before* multiplying, rather
than after — same result either way.

{{< fillin
  question="Multiply, and write the answer in simplified form: $-\tfrac{4}{7} \cdot \left(-\tfrac{5}{8}\right)$"
  answer="\frac{5}{14}"
  answerDisplay="$\tfrac{5}{14}$"
  hint="Same signs give a positive product. Look for a common factor of $4$ before multiplying."
>}}

**Example.** Multiply, and write the answer in simplified form:
$-\tfrac{14}{15} \cdot \tfrac{20}{21}$.

The product is negative (opposite signs). Since $7$ is a common factor of
$14$ and $21$, and $5$ is a common factor of $20$ and $15$, remove them
before multiplying:

$$-\frac{14}{15} \cdot \frac{20}{21} = -\frac{2 \cdot 4}{3 \cdot 3} = -\frac{8}{9}$$

{{< fillin
  question="Multiply, and write the answer in simplified form: $-\tfrac{10}{28} \cdot \tfrac{8}{15}$"
  answer="-\frac{4}{21}"
  answerDisplay="$-\tfrac{4}{21}$"
  hint="Look for common factors between $10$ and $15$, and between $28$ and $8$, before multiplying."
>}}

To multiply a fraction by an integer, it helps to write the integer as a
fraction — any integer $a$ can be written as $\tfrac{a}{1}$.

**Example.** Multiply, and write the answer in simplified form:
$\tfrac{12}{5}(-20x)$.

Write $-20x$ as $\tfrac{-20x}{1}$. The product is negative. Multiplying
and removing common factors of $4$ and $5$:

$$\frac{12}{5}(-20x) = -48x$$

  answer="8"
  hint="Write $56$ as $\tfrac{56}{1}$, then multiply and simplify."
>}}

## Find reciprocals

The fractions $\tfrac{2}{3}$ and $\tfrac{3}{2}$ are related in a special
way — they look like upside-down (inverted) versions of one another, and
$\tfrac{2}{3} \cdot \tfrac{3}{2} = 1$. Such pairs of numbers are called
**reciprocals**.

{{< callout type="info" >}}
  **Reciprocal.** The reciprocal of the fraction $\tfrac{a}{b}$ is
  $\tfrac{b}{a}$, where $a \neq 0$ and $b \neq 0$. A number and its
  reciprocal have a product of $1$:

  $$\frac{a}{b} \cdot \frac{b}{a} = 1$$
{{< /callout >}}

To find the reciprocal, keep the same sign and invert the fraction — since
a number and its reciprocal multiply to a positive $1$, they must have the
same sign. The number $0$ has no reciprocal, since no number $r$ satisfies
$0 \cdot r = 1$.

**Example.** Find the reciprocal of each number, and check that the
product with its original is $1$: (a) $\tfrac{4}{9}$; (b) $-\tfrac{1}{6}$;
(c) $-\tfrac{14}{5}$; (d) $7$.

(a) The reciprocal of $\tfrac{4}{9}$ is $\tfrac{9}{4}$. Check:
$\tfrac{4}{9} \cdot \tfrac{9}{4} = 1$.

(b) The reciprocal of $-\tfrac{1}{6}$ is $-6$. Check:
$-\tfrac{1}{6} \cdot (-6) = 1$.

(c) The reciprocal of $-\tfrac{14}{5}$ is $-\tfrac{5}{14}$. Check:
$-\tfrac{14}{5} \cdot \left(-\tfrac{5}{14}\right) = 1$.

(d) Write $7$ as $\tfrac{7}{1}$; its reciprocal is $\tfrac{1}{7}$. Check:
$7 \cdot \tfrac{1}{7} = 1$.

  question="Find the reciprocal of $-\tfrac{11}{4}$."
  answer="-\frac{4}{11}"
  answerDisplay="$-\tfrac{4}{11}$"
  hint="Keep the same sign and invert the fraction."
>}}

{{< fillin
  question="Find the reciprocal of $14$."
  answer="\frac{1}{14}"
  answerDisplay="$\tfrac{1}{14}$"
  hint="Write $14$ as $\tfrac{14}{1}$ first, then invert."
>}}

Recall from the integers chapter that opposite and absolute value behave
differently from reciprocal: the opposite of a number flips its sign, the
absolute value is never negative, and the reciprocal keeps the same sign
but inverts the fraction.

## Divide fractions

Why is $12 \div 3 = 4$? Because there are $4$ groups of $3$ in $12$. In
the same way, to divide fractions we can ask how many of the divisor fit
into the dividend. Using fraction tiles, lining up half- and sixth-tiles
shows there are three $\tfrac{1}{6}$ tiles in $\tfrac{1}{2}$, so
$\tfrac{1}{2} \div \tfrac{1}{6} = 3$.

Notice that $\tfrac{1}{2} \div \tfrac{1}{6} = 3$ and also
$\tfrac{1}{2} \cdot \tfrac{6}{1} = 3$ — dividing by $\tfrac{1}{6}$ gives
the same result as multiplying by its reciprocal, $\tfrac{6}{1}$. This
leads to the procedure for fraction division.

{{< callout type="info" >}}
  **Fraction division.** If $a$, $b$, $c$, and $d$ are numbers where
  $b \neq 0$, $c \neq 0$, and $d \neq 0$, then

  $$\frac{a}{b} \div \frac{c}{d} = \frac{a}{b} \cdot \frac{d}{c}$$

  To divide fractions, multiply the first fraction by the reciprocal of
  the second.
{{< /callout >}}

**Example.** Divide, and write the answer in simplified form:
$\tfrac{2}{5} \div \left(-\tfrac{3}{7}\right)$.

Multiply by the reciprocal of the second fraction:
$\tfrac{2}{5} \cdot \left(-\tfrac{7}{3}\right)$. The product is negative:

$$\frac{2}{5} \div \left(-\frac{3}{7}\right) = -\frac{14}{15}$$

{{< fillin
  question="Divide, and write the answer in simplified form: $\tfrac{3}{7} \div \left(-\tfrac{2}{3}\right)$"
  answer="-\frac{9}{14}"
  answerDisplay="$-\tfrac{9}{14}$"
  hint="Multiply the first fraction by the reciprocal of the second, then determine the sign."
>}}

**Example.** Divide, and write the answer in simplified form:
$-\tfrac{3}{4} \div \left(-\tfrac{7}{8}\right)$.

Multiply by the reciprocal of the second: $-\tfrac{3}{4} \cdot
\left(-\tfrac{8}{7}\right)$. Both signs are negative, so the product is
positive. Rewrite to show the common factor of $4$ and remove it:

$$-\frac{3}{4} \div \left(-\frac{7}{8}\right) = \frac{6}{7}$$

{{< fillin
  question="Divide, and write the answer in simplified form: $-\tfrac{2}{3} \div \left(-\tfrac{5}{6}\right)$"
  answer="\frac{4}{5}"
  answerDisplay="$\tfrac{4}{5}$"
  hint="Multiply by the reciprocal of the second fraction. Both are negative, so the result is positive."
>}}

**Example.** Divide, and write the answer in simplified form:
$\tfrac{7}{18} \div \tfrac{14}{27}$.

Multiply by the reciprocal: $\tfrac{7}{18} \cdot \tfrac{27}{14}$.
Rewriting to show common factors of $7$, $9$, and $2$ and removing them:

$$\frac{7}{18} \div \frac{14}{27} = \frac{3}{4}$$

## Key terms

**simplified fraction** — a fraction with no common factors, other than
$1$, in its numerator and denominator. **fraction multiplication** — to
multiply two fractions, multiply the numerators and multiply the
denominators, $\tfrac{a}{b} \cdot \tfrac{c}{d} = \tfrac{ac}{bd}$.
**reciprocal** — the fraction $\tfrac{b}{a}$, obtained by inverting
$\tfrac{a}{b}$ ($a \neq 0$, $b \neq 0$); a number and its reciprocal
multiply to $1$. **fraction division** — to divide by a fraction,
multiply by its reciprocal, $\tfrac{a}{b} \div \tfrac{c}{d} =
\tfrac{a}{b} \cdot \tfrac{d}{c}$.
