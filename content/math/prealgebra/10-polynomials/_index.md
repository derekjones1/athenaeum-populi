Expressions known as polynomials are used widely in algebra. Applications of
these expressions are essential to many careers, including economists,
engineers, and scientists. In this chapter, we will find out what
polynomials are and how to manipulate them through basic mathematical
operations.

## Sections

- **Add and Subtract Polynomials** — identifying polynomials, monomials,
  binomials, and trinomials, determining the degree of a polynomial, adding
  and subtracting monomials and polynomials, and evaluating a polynomial for
  a given value.
- **Use Multiplication Properties of Exponents** — using the product,
  power, and product-to-a-power properties of exponents, and simplifying
  expressions using a combination of these properties.
- **Multiply Polynomials** — multiplying a polynomial by a monomial,
  multiplying a binomial by a binomial, and multiplying a trinomial by a
  binomial.
- **Divide Monomials** — simplifying expressions using the quotient
  property of exponents, the zero exponent property, and the quotient to a
  power property, and dividing monomials.
- **Integer Exponents and Scientific Notation** — using the definition of a
  negative exponent, converting between scientific notation and decimal
  form, and multiplying and dividing numbers written in scientific notation.
- **Introduction to Factoring Polynomials** — finding the greatest common
  factor of two or more expressions, and factoring the greatest common
  factor from a polynomial.
