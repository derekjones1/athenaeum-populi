We are surrounded by all sorts of geometry. Architects use geometry to
design buildings. Artists create vivid images out of colorful geometric
shapes. Street signs, automobiles, and product packaging all take advantage
of geometric properties. In this chapter, we will begin by considering a
formal approach to solving problems and use it to solve a variety of common
problems, including making decisions about money. Then we will explore
geometry and relate it to everyday situations, using the problem-solving
strategy we develop.

## Sections

- **Use a Problem Solving Strategy** — approaching word problems with a
  positive attitude, using a problem-solving strategy for word problems, and
  solving number problems.
- **Solve Money Applications** — solving coin word problems and applying the
  problem-solving strategy to more money applications.
- **Use Properties of Angles, Triangles, and the Pythagorean Theorem** —
  using the properties of angles, and the properties of triangles, and using
  the Pythagorean Theorem.
- **Use Properties of Rectangles, Triangles, and Trapezoids** — using the
  properties of rectangles, triangles, and trapezoids to find perimeter and
  area.
- **Solve Geometry Applications: Circles and Irregular Figures** — using the
  properties of circles, and finding the area of irregular figures.
- **Solve Geometry Applications: Volume and Surface Area** — finding the
  volume and surface area of rectangular solids, spheres, cylinders, and
  cones, and finding the volume of composite solids.
- **Solve a Formula for a Specific Variable** — solving a formula for a
  specific variable in the categories of geometry, distance-rate-time, and
  temperature.
