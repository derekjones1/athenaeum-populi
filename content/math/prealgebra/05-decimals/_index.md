Gasoline price changes all the time. It might go down for a period of time,
but then it usually rises again. One thing that stays the same is that the
price is not usually a whole number. Instead, it is shown using a decimal
point to describe the cost in dollars and cents. We use decimal numbers all
the time, especially when dealing with money. In this chapter, we explore
decimal numbers and how to perform operations using them.

## Sections

- **Decimals** — naming and writing decimals, converting decimals to
  fractions or mixed numbers, locating decimals on the number line,
  ordering decimals, and rounding decimals.
- **Decimal Operations** — adding, subtracting, multiplying, and dividing
  decimals, multiplying and dividing by powers of ten, and using decimals
  in money applications.
- **Decimals and Fractions** — converting fractions to decimals (including
  repeating decimals), ordering decimals and fractions, simplifying
  expressions with the order of operations, and finding the circumference
  and area of circles.
- **Solve Equations with Decimals** — determining whether a decimal is a
  solution of an equation, solving equations with decimals, and
  translating word sentences to equations.
- **Averages and Probability** — calculating the mean, median, and mode of
  a set of numbers, and using the basic definition of probability.
- **Ratios and Rate** — writing ratios and rates as fractions, finding
  unit rates and unit price, and translating phrases to expressions with
  fractions.
- **Simplify and Use Square Roots** — simplifying expressions with square
  roots, estimating and approximating square roots, simplifying variable
  expressions with square roots, and using square roots in applications.
