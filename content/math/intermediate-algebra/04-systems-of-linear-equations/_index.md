Climb into your car. Put on your seatbelt. Choose your destination and
then&hellip;relax. That's right &mdash; you don't have to do anything else
because you are in an autonomous car, or one that navigates its way to your
destination. No cars are fully autonomous at the moment, so you still
theoretically need to have your hands on the wheel. Self-driving cars may
help ease traffic congestion, prevent accidents, and lower pollution. The
technology is thanks to computer programmers who are developing software to
control the navigation of the car. These programmers rely on their
understanding of mathematics, including relationships between equations. In
this chapter, you will learn how to solve systems of linear equations in
different ways and use them to analyze real-world situations.

## Sections

- **Solve Systems of Linear Equations with Two Variables** — determining
  whether an ordered pair is a solution of a system of equations, solving a
  system of linear equations by graphing, solving a system of equations by
  substitution, solving a system of equations by elimination, and choosing
  the most convenient method to solve a system of linear equations.
- **Solve Applications with Systems of Equations** — solving direct
  translation applications, geometry applications, and uniform motion
  applications with systems of equations.
- **Solve Mixture Applications with Systems of Equations** — solving
  mixture applications and interest applications with systems of equations.
- **Solve Systems of Equations with Three Variables** — determining whether
  an ordered triple is a solution of a system of three linear equations,
  solving a system of linear equations with three variables, and solving
  applications using systems of linear equations with three variables.
- **Solve Systems of Equations Using Matrices** — writing the augmented
  matrix for a system of equations, using row operations on a matrix, and
  solving systems of equations using matrices.
- **Solve Systems of Equations Using Determinants** — evaluating the
  determinant of a 2&times;2 matrix and a 3&times;3 matrix, using Cramer's
  rule to solve systems of equations, and identifying inconsistent systems
  and systems with dependent equations.
- **Graphing Systems of Linear Inequalities** — determining whether an
  ordered pair is a solution of a system of linear inequalities and solving
  a system of linear inequalities by graphing.
