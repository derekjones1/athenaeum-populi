This is the rung after Elementary Algebra: a second course that carries the
core skills forward into the language of functions. It opens by consolidating
the foundations — real numbers and their properties — then moves through
solving linear equations, graphs and functions, systems, polynomials,
factoring, rational and radical expressions, quadratics, exponential and
logarithmic functions, conics, and finally sequences and series.

{{< callout type="info" >}}
  **Prerequisites:** Elementary Algebra, or comfort with solving linear
  equations, graphing lines, and working with polynomials and factoring. This
  is advisory, not a gate — start here if the earlier material already feels
  familiar.
{{< /callout >}}

## Chapters

This book is organized into twelve chapters, each broken into short sections
you can work through in order.

- **Foundations** — the language of algebra, integers, fractions, decimals,
  and the properties of real numbers.
- **Solving Linear Equations** — a general strategy, problem solving,
  formulas, mixture and uniform motion applications, and linear, compound,
  and absolute value inequalities.
- **Graphs and Functions** — the rectangular coordinate system, slope,
  finding equations of lines, graphing linear inequalities, and relations and
  functions.
- **Systems of Linear Equations** — solving systems in two and three
  variables, applications, and solving systems using matrices and
  determinants.
- **Polynomial and Polynomial Functions** — adding, subtracting, and
  multiplying polynomials, exponent properties, polynomial functions,
  dividing, and factoring by greatest common factor and grouping.
- **Factoring** — factoring trinomials, special products, a general strategy,
  and solving quadratic equations by factoring.
- **Rational Expressions and Functions** — simplifying, operating on, and
  solving rational expressions and equations, rational functions, and
  variation.
- **Roots and Radicals** — simplifying and operating on radicals, rational
  exponents, solving radical equations, and complex numbers.
- **Quadratic Equations and Functions** — the square root property,
  completing the square, the quadratic formula, quadratic form, graphing, and
  quadratic inequalities.
- **Exponential and Logarithmic Functions** — inverse functions, exponential
  and logarithmic functions, the properties of logarithms, and solving
  exponential and logarithmic equations.
- **Conics** — distance and midpoint, circles, parabolas, ellipses,
  hyperbolas, and solving systems of nonlinear equations.
- **Sequences, Series, and the Binomial Theorem** — sequences, arithmetic and
  geometric sequences and series, and the Binomial Theorem.
