We have seen that the graphs of linear equations are straight lines. Graphs of
other types of equations, called polynomial equations, are curves, like the
outline of a suspension bridge. Architects use polynomials to design the shape
of a bridge like this and to draw the blueprints for it. Engineers use
polynomials to calculate the stress on the bridge's supports to ensure they are
strong enough for the intended load. In this chapter, you will explore
operations with and properties of polynomials.

## Sections

- **Add and Subtract Polynomials** — identifying polynomials, monomials,
  binomials, and trinomials; determining the degree of polynomials; adding and
  subtracting monomials and polynomials; and evaluating a polynomial for a
  given value.
- **Use Multiplication Properties of Exponents** — simplifying expressions with
  exponents, and using the product, power, and product-to-a-power properties of
  exponents together.
- **Multiply Polynomials** — multiplying a polynomial by a monomial,
  multiplying two binomials (Distributive, FOIL, and Vertical methods),
  multiplying a trinomial by a binomial.
- **Special Products** — squaring a binomial using the Binomial Squares
  pattern, and multiplying conjugates using the Product of Conjugates pattern.
- **Divide Monomials** — simplifying expressions using the Quotient Property of
  Exponents, the Zero Exponent Property, and the Quotient to a Power Property,
  and dividing monomials.
- **Divide Polynomials** — dividing a polynomial by a monomial and dividing a
  polynomial by a binomial.
- **Integer Exponents and Scientific Notation** — using the definition of a
  negative exponent, simplifying expressions with integer exponents, converting
  between decimal and scientific notation, and multiplying and dividing in
  scientific notation.
