The trajectories of fireworks are modeled by quadratic equations. The
equations can be used to predict the maximum height of a firework and the
number of seconds it will take from launch to explosion. In this chapter, we
will study the properties of quadratic equations, solve them, graph them, and
see how they are applied as models of various situations.

## Chapter Outline

- **10.1** Solve Quadratic Equations Using the Square Root Property
- **10.2** Solve Quadratic Equations by Completing the Square
- **10.3** Solve Quadratic Equations Using the Quadratic Formula
- **10.4** Solve Applications Modeled by Quadratic Equations
- **10.5** Graphing Quadratic Equations in Two Variables
