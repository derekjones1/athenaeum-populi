Suppose a stone falls from the edge of a cliff. The number of feet the stone
has dropped after $t$ seconds can be found by multiplying $16$ times the square
of $t$. But to calculate the number of seconds it would take the stone to hit
the land below, we need to use a square root. In this chapter, we will
introduce and apply the properties of square roots, and extend these concepts
to higher-order roots and rational exponents.

## Sections

- **Simplify and Use Square Roots** — simplifying expressions with square
  roots, estimating square roots, approximating them with a calculator, and
  simplifying variable expressions with square roots.
- **Simplify Square Roots** — using the Product Property and the Quotient
  Property to simplify square roots, including those with variables and
  coefficients.
- **Add and Subtract Square Roots** — combining like square roots, and
  simplifying first so that radicals can be combined.
- **Multiply Square Roots** — multiplying square roots, using polynomial
  multiplication patterns with square roots, and finding special products
  involving conjugates.
- **Divide Square Roots** — dividing square roots and rationalizing a
  denominator, including denominators that contain a sum or difference.
- **Solve Equations with Square Roots** — solving radical equations, checking
  for extraneous solutions, and using radical equations in applications.
- **Higher Roots** — simplifying, using, and combining cube roots and other
  higher-order roots, including expressions with variables.
- **Rational Exponents** — simplifying expressions with rational exponents of
  the forms $a^{1/n}$ and $a^{m/n}$, and using the properties of exponents
  with them.
