Just like a building needs a firm foundation to support it, a study of
algebra needs a firm foundation too. This chapter reviews arithmetic with
whole numbers, integers, fractions, and decimals, so that you have a solid
base for the algebra to come.

## Sections

- **Introduction to Whole Numbers** — place value, multiples, divisibility
  tests, and prime factorization.
- **Use the Language of Algebra** — variables, algebraic expressions, and
  the order of operations.
- **Add and Subtract Integers** — signed numbers and their operations.
- **Multiply and Divide Integers** — signed-number rules for multiplication
  and division.
- **Visualize Fractions** — equivalent fractions, simplifying, and
  multiplying and dividing fractions.
- **Add and Subtract Fractions** — combining fractions with like and unlike
  denominators.
- **Decimals** — operations on decimals and converting between decimals,
  fractions, and percents.
- **The Real Numbers** — rational and irrational numbers and where they sit
  on the number line.
- **Properties of Real Numbers** — the commutative, associative,
  distributive, and identity properties.
- **Systems of Measurement** — the U.S. and metric systems, and converting
  between them.
