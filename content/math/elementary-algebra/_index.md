This is the next rung after Prealgebra: a first full course in algebra. It
starts by consolidating the arithmetic foundations — real numbers and their
properties — and builds from there through solving equations, graphing lines,
working with polynomials, factoring, rational and radical expressions, and
finally quadratic equations.

{{< callout type="info" >}}
  **Prerequisites:** Prealgebra, or comfort with integers, fractions,
  decimals, and the basic language of algebra. This is advisory, not a
  gate — start here if the foundations already feel familiar.
{{< /callout >}}

## Chapters

This book is organized into ten chapters, each broken into short sections you
can work through in order.

- **Foundations** — whole numbers, the language of algebra, integers,
  fractions, decimals, the real numbers, their properties, and systems of
  measurement.
- **Solving Linear Equations and Inequalities** — the properties of equality,
  general strategies for solving, formulas, and linear inequalities.
- **Math Models** — problem-solving strategies and applications: percents,
  mixtures, geometry, uniform motion, and inequalities.
- **Graphs** — the rectangular coordinate system, graphing linear equations,
  intercepts, slope, slope-intercept form, and equations of lines.
- **Systems of Linear Equations** — solving by graphing, substitution, and
  elimination, plus applications and systems of inequalities.
- **Polynomials** — adding, subtracting, and multiplying polynomials,
  exponent properties, dividing, and scientific notation.
- **Factoring** — greatest common factor, factoring trinomials and special
  products, a general strategy, and quadratic equations by factoring.
- **Rational Expressions and Equations** — simplifying, operating on, and
  solving rational expressions, proportions, and variation.
- **Roots and Radicals** — square roots, operations on radicals, solving
  radical equations, higher roots, and rational exponents.
- **Quadratic Equations** — the square root property, completing the square,
  the quadratic formula, applications, and graphing.
