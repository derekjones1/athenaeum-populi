Mathematical formulas model phenomena in every facet of our lives — they
explain events and predict outcomes in fields from transportation to
business to medicine. This chapter applies the equation-solving skills from
Chapter 2 to word problems in a variety of real situations.

## Sections

- **Use a Problem-Solving Strategy** — approaching word problems with a
  positive attitude, a repeatable strategy for translating them into
  equations, and applying it to number problems.
- **Solve Percent Applications** — translating percent statements into
  equations, discount and mark-up, and simple interest.
- **Solve Mixture Applications** — coin and ticket problems, mixing
  different-priced items, and mixing percent concentrations.
- **Solve Geometry Applications: Triangles, Rectangles, and the Pythagorean
  Theorem** — angles of triangles, properties of rectangles, and the
  Pythagorean Theorem.
- **Solve Uniform Motion Applications** — using $d = rt$ to solve
  distance-rate-time problems.
- **Solve Applications with Linear Inequalities** — translating phrases
  into inequalities and solving applications with them.
