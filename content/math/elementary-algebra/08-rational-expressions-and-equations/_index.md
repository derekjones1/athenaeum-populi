Like rowing a boat, riding a bicycle is a situation in which going in one
direction — downhill — is easy, but going in the opposite direction — uphill —
can be more work. The trip to reach a destination may be quick, but the return
trip, whether upstream or uphill, will take longer. Rational equations are used
to model situations like these. In this chapter, you will work with rational
expressions, solve rational equations, and use them to solve problems in a
variety of applications.

## Sections

- **Simplify Rational Expressions** — determining the values for which a
  rational expression is undefined, evaluating rational expressions, and
  simplifying them, including expressions with opposite factors.
- **Multiply and Divide Rational Expressions** — multiplying and dividing
  rational expressions and multiplying and dividing a rational function.
- **Add and Subtract Rational Expressions with a Common Denominator** — adding
  and subtracting rational expressions that share a denominator, then
  simplifying the result.
- **Add and Subtract Rational Expressions with Unlike Denominators** — finding
  the least common denominator, rewriting each expression over it, and adding
  or subtracting.
- **Simplify Complex Rational Expressions** — simplifying a complex rational
  expression by writing it as division and by using the least common
  denominator.
- **Solve Rational Equations** — solving rational equations, checking for
  extraneous solutions, and solving a rational equation for a specified
  variable.
- **Solve Proportion and Similar Figure Applications** — solving proportions
  and applications modeled by proportions and by similar figures.
- **Solve Uniform Motion and Work Applications** — solving applications
  involving uniform motion and work using rational equations.
- **Use Direct and Inverse Variation** — solving direct variation problems,
  inverse variation problems, and applications of each.
