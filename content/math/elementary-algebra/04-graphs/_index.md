Graphs are found in all areas of our lives — from commercials showing which
cell phone carrier provides the best coverage, to bank statements and news
articles, to the boardroom of major corporations. This chapter studies the
rectangular coordinate system, which is the basis for most consumer graphs.
We will look at linear graphs, slopes of lines, equations of lines, and
linear inequalities.

## Sections

- **Use the Rectangular Coordinate System** — plotting points in the
  $xy$-plane, verifying solutions to an equation in two variables,
  completing a table of solutions, and finding solutions to a linear
  equation in two variables.
- **Graph Linear Equations in Two Variables** — graphing by plotting points,
  graphing vertical and horizontal lines, and graphing using a table of
  solutions.
- **Graph with Intercepts** — identifying the $x$- and $y$-intercepts of a
  line, graphing a line using its intercepts, and choosing the most
  convenient method to graph a line.
- **Understand Slope of a Line** — finding the slope of a line from its
  graph, from two points, and using the slope formula; graphing a line
  given a point and the slope.
- **Use the Slope-Intercept Form of an Equation of a Line** — recognizing
  slope and $y$-intercept from an equation, graphing with the
  slope-intercept form, and identifying parallel and perpendicular lines.
- **Find the Equation of a Line** — finding an equation given the slope and
  a point, given two points, or given a point and a parallel or
  perpendicular line.
- **Graphs of Linear Inequalities** — verifying solutions to a linear
  inequality in two variables and graphing linear inequalities in two
  variables.
